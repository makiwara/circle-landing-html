Conceptual Thinking Circle

Aim & Function

The Circle is both a time and a space dedicated to tackle deep issues of design and to grow over our selves.

The sole purpose of the Circle is to wake a taste for conceptual approach in its members.

The Circle is open to new members who agree to obide by the Rules and to pay rather modest regular due.

It is adviced to take these rules in consideration before application.

Study Of Metaphora

Members of the Circle will research the connection between products with its interfaces and objects surrounding in real life. There are three topics, two colloquia and number of studies.

Topic #1

Introduction to product metaphorization. History of interfaces in the light of metaphor. Adoption of Hi-tec from the past. Controls and pictograms. Incentive for metaphor. Metaphor vs. skeumorphism. Metaphor beyond designer perception.

Topic #2

Function of metaphorae in human perception and cognition. Boundary of metaphors. Grounding metaphor. Virtual metaphor vs. surface metaphor. Synthesis vis-a-vi metaphor. Analysis vis-a-vi metaphor. Feature-centered approach and its boundaries. Risks of metaphorical thinking.

Topic #3

Metaphora design. ‘Old-time thing’ mental finesse. ‘Virtual perception’ mental finesse. Analitical approach to metaphor. Multiple metaphors. Description and documentation of metaphors.

Product Mentation

Members of the Circle will practice mental finesse, research different techniques of conception and develop their own ones. There are three lectures, a pair of colloquia and some studies.

Topic #1

Conception, design and engineering. History of concept engineering schools. Methodology of design. Mental finesse methodology. Connection between mental finesse and school technique. Concept efficiency metrics.

Topic #2

‘Reference denial’ mental finesse. ‘Reverse the opposite’ mental finesse. ‘There is no interface‘ mental finesse. Concept of centricity. App-centricity, content-centricity, interaction-centricity. ‘Everything is X’ mental finesse. ‘Stretch conflict’ finesse.

Topic #3

Design from motivation, desire and underlying emotivity. Completists, recommendations, secrets, anecdotes, curiosity. Search and selection of appropriate motive. Implicit motivation in communication, interaction, visual language. ‘Locate desire’ mental finesse.

Visual Language

Members of the Circle will work on issues of non-verbal speech conducted by means of design during number of topics, colloquia and studies.

Topic #1

Product visual language in general. History of visual language. History of visual language design. Mutual and reciprocal influence between different schools of visual language. Language elements: form, texture, tectonics, composition, font, illustration, controls.

Topic #2

State of art: most recognized visual languages deconstructed. Trend-oriented communication vs. niche language. Language habit vs. innovation and novoyaz. Analytical approach to visual language. Use of visual language consultant, art-director, researcher.

Topic #3

Real objects in visual language design. Physical notions of space, material, time and interaction. Borders of visual language. Relative learning curves in visual language. Visual language as means for interface reduction. Synthetical approach to visual language design.

The Circle is a cycle of reading, listening, research, practice and reflexion.

Practices

Each topic is covered by a vast bibliography. Members are expected to study given references.

Lectures provide members with insight that allows to build their own perspective on the topic.

Members are expected to perform some research around their field of interest and turn-in generalised results.

Schoolmen provide members with a number of studies to grind their skills.

To reflect is to thoroughly inspect and discuss one’s results in order to dis-cover and to accept one’s new self.
